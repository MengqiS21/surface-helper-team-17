SurfaceHelper

A cleaner helper specially designed for flat surfaces. Small, modular, and friendly.

The Concept

Cleaning the stove top every time after cooking is a tiring chore, and fresh spills often harden if left unattended. We wanted to address this common issue by creating a solution that acts as a dedicated assistant for your kitchen surfaces.

SurfaceHelper is a prototyped small desktop cleaning robot designed for removing light stains on stovetops and countertops. Instead of full autonomy, it focuses on form, mechanism, internal layout, and a simple user-controlled cleaning action. Users can move it by hand or via a basic remote control, and it utilizes a detachable cleaning pad or brush. Process & Iterations

Our journey from sketches to the final "dress-up" robot. We learned that early constraint-setting and small customization options can make the product both more efficient to build and more engaging to use.

Step 1

Sketching & Concept

We explored different forms and interactions on paper before committing to any 3D work. Sketching helped us clarify the main concept and visualize how the user would hold and operate the device.

Step 2

Component Testing

We printed and tested small parts first to check size, tolerance, and motion. Although we didn't use all these exact parts in the hi-fi model, this step was crucial for understanding the physical constraints of our mechanisms.

Steps 3 & 4

Low-Fidelity & Iteration

We built a rough full-body model to see the overall volume. However, we realized our first low-fi shape did not match the internal structure we needed, so the mechanism could not fit well. We adjusted the overall form and proportions in the next iteration to better support the gears and future decoration, eventually adopting a new wheelbase.

Step 5

Modeling & Mechanism

We wanted to add more playful variety. To address the "boring" appearance of the initial structure, we designed an interchangeable robot front. This allows users to personalize the robot and creates a more engaging interaction, making cleaning no longer a boring process.

Step 6

Decoration & Finish

We experimented with surface and color to express the project's theme. We utilized spray printing to cover the original yellow material color, creating a cohesive aesthetic that matched our interchangeable "dress-up" covers (like the marine theme or robotic acrylic mosaic).
